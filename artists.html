There are many different types of art, and many famous artists within each genre. Trying to name the best artist of all time is nearly impossible because each variant of art is so unique that it's hard to compare it against others. Even within a single genre, numerous artists have different styles and use different materials, which makes it so that judging artists is quite a difficult task.



That being said, below is a list of some of the well-known artists in history. The following people were chosen so that not all would end up being painters or sculptors. Let's take a look at some of the most renowned artists to have ever walked the Earth.




Michelangelo


Michelangelo, born in 1475 as Michelangelo di Lodovico Buonarroti Simoni, was an Italian painter, architect sculptor and poet. Some his most famous works include the frescoes inside the Sistine Chapel and a sculpture of David. Michelangelo is arguably one of the most famous and talented artists in history. His work was revered by many, and he was even considered the greatest artist during his lifetime.

Leonardo da Vinci


Leonardo da Vinci, born near Florence in 1452, was an Italian painter, sculptor, architect, draftsmen and engineer. He's famous for his paintings Mona Lisa and The Last Supper, as well as his drawing Vitruvian Man. da Vinci was considered to be one of the greatest men on Earth during the Renaissance. His drawings and ideas were centuries ahead of his time, and as a result, da Vinci has gained global fame for over 5 centuries.

Akira Yoshizawa


Akira Yoshizawa, born in the Tochigi prefecture in 1911, was a Japanese master of origami. His art wasn't conventional, but certainly made a lasting impact on the world. Yoshizawa is said to have revived the ancient Japanese craft of origami and created global interest in the art. He was able to use single sheets of paper to make intricate designs. Yoshizawa also invented many different origami techniques, and completely revolutionized the art. He received the Order of the Rising Sun (prestigious Japanese award) in 1983.

Zaha Hadid


Zaha Hadid, born in Baghdad in 1950, was an Iraqi-British architect. She was famous for her stunning designs, some of which include the Heydar Aliyev Cultural Center, the Galaxy Soho Beijing and the Guangzhou Opera House. Her achievements were considered to be spectacular considering the fact that her field of expertise was dominated by men. She was awarded the Pritzker Architecture Prize and the Stirling prize, and was made a Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire in 2012.
